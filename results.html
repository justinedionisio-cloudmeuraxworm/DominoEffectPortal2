Now that the simulation is over, here's what actually gave that message away.

No single control in this simulation could have stopped the phishing message from being convincing — that's a human judgment call, not a network setting. VPN and Firewall protect different things (traffic privacy and suspicious outbound activity) that matter for other kinds of threats, even though neither stopped this specific credential theft. MFA is what actually limited the damage once the credentials were already gone. Layered security means every layer still matters — just not always for the reason you'd expect.
